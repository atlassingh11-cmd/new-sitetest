Overview

What The Edit is — and who it is for

The Edit at d3 puts residential towers inside Dubai Design District — the 32-million-square-foot creative quarter on Dubai Creek that houses the region's design, fashion and creative industries, a few minutes upstream from Downtown. Meraas, the master developer behind Bluewaters Residences and the Bvlgari Residences, is building three connected towers described as a "dynamic triad": one- to four-bedroom residences, three signature penthouses, sky gardens carved into the architecture, and each tower themed around a different life — social and creative in one, wellness in the second, family in the third.

The position is the argument. d3 sits near Downtown with stated drive times of six minutes to Dubai Mall, seven to Downtown and City Walk, ten to Business Bay and fifteen to DXB — while facing the water and the Ras Al Khor sanctuary skyline rather than the traffic. The district's masterplan adds a waterfront promenade connected to Dubai Creek by electric abras, a sports and mangrove park, galleries, studios and Soho-style low-rise quarters.

What the brochure does not print: prices, unit sizes, unit counts, floor counts or a completion date. Availability and terms move per release — start there, not at the render.

Off-plan — construction status and handover timelines should be confirmed with Iffy before committing.

Design & Specification

Architecture and interiors

Interiors follow the same brief: natural light, considered materials, open plans, custom-built wardrobes and refined finishes, with panoramic glazing framing the water and skyline. Investment Case

The honest investment picture

The Edit's case rests on scarcity of address: d3 is an established employment and lifestyle district with very little residential stock inside it, and this is a master developer building on the district's waterfront. Rental demand can draw on d3's own creative workforce plus the Downtown-DIFC commuter pool — tenants who want water, calm and a six-minute run to the Mall. That demand thesis is credible but unproven at tower scale until handover.

Growth depends on d3's residential build-out and how much stock follows this project: the masterplan contemplates more homes in Soho-style quarters and the Creekside district, so first-mover pricing versus later phases matters. Meraas's delivered record — Bluewaters, City Walk, the Bvlgari island — is the strongest developer comfort in this set, but no prices, dates or sizes are published yet, so the whole underwriting starts with the current release terms. Returns are not guaranteed; verify service-charge expectations and short-let rules per tower before modelling income.

Iffy's View

Iffy's view on The Edit

The Edit is the most Downtown-adjacent value conversation I can offer buyers who find Downtown itself overheated and Business Bay too uneven. It suits urban end-users and design-led buyers first, penthouse hunters second, and investors who want a differentiated waterfront story rather than commodity stock. It will not suit spreadsheet-first yield buyers — the Bay does that cheaper — and anyone allergic to phase-released pricing should wait for the numbers. Before reserving: get the price list, the view line of the specific stack, which tower's amenity theme fits your life, and the contractual dates in writing.

Where is The Edit at d3?
