The Meriva Collection by Ellington

Ellington's most ambitious waterfront community — twin eleven-storey towers within a five-building beachfront masterplan on Dubai Islands, with a seafront hotel, sea-facing residences from 832 to 4,648 sq ft, freehold title and a published payment plan.

Overview

What Meriva is — and who it is for

The Meriva Collection is Ellington's flagship move onto Dubai Islands: a beachfront community the developer describes as its most ambitious waterfront project, combining five residential buildings, a signature residence and a seafront hotel along the shoreline. This release covers the twin towers — basement, ground, ten residential floors and rooftop each — positioned for sea views, natural light and privacy, above a pedestrianised ground plane with 33 retail units, cascading water and layered landscaping.

Unusually for an island launch, the paperwork is specific: freehold ownership, an anticipated Q2 2030 completion, an anticipated service charge of AED 18 per sq ft, published unit sizes from 832 sq ft one-beds to a 4,648 sq ft four-bedroom penthouse, and a printed payment plan with 20% down and 30% at completion. That transparency lets a buyer actually underwrite the wait — which, on a 2030 horizon, is the honest core of this purchase.

Meriva suits early-cycle buyers who want new beachfront at island pricing with the numbers on the table, and design-led end-users buying two to three years ahead of need. It is not for anyone needing rent or keys soon.

Location

Where Meriva sits — and what that means

Meriva sits on the shoreline of Dubai Islands, Nakheel's five-island masterplan off the Deira coast. The brochure prints no drive times, so treat these as approximate and traffic-dependent: the 2025 bridge connections put Gold Souq metro around ten minutes away, DXB Airport roughly 15–20 minutes, and Downtown 20–30. The islands themselves are early-cycle — beaches and resorts operate today, while neighbourhood retail, schools and clinics arrive with the residential phases. Design & Specification

Architecture and interiors

Ellington's design language here is sea-led: layered curved architecture stepping back from the shoreline, interiors in soft greys, warm taupe and natural beige with light wood cabinetry, grey marble surfaces and champagne metal accents, and full-height glazing pulling the water into living rooms and bedrooms. Published parking allocations run from one space for one-beds to three for penthouses, with three tenant lifts and two visitor lifts per building. Furnishing status and smart-home provision are not printed — confirm with the specification pack.

Investment Case

The honest investment picture

Meriva's case is early-cycle beachfront with unusually complete disclosure. The published payment plan back-loads 30% to a 2030 completion, which spreads capital thinly across the build — attractive for buyers who want island exposure without full outlay, but it also means committing years before the district's rental market exists. No rental history is available on Dubai Islands to underwrite against; resort demand and Deira's mainland market are directional signals, not comparables.

Growth depends on the islands maturing on plan and on Ellington's delivery — the developer has a strong design-led record in Dubai, though this is its most ambitious waterfront scale to date. The five-building masterplan plus a hotel means later phases will follow this one; entry pricing versus those phases is a question worth asking. Weigh the AED 18/sq ft anticipated service charge in any yield model, verify short-let permissions per tower, and treat every projection as unproven until handovers generate real data. No returns are guaranteed.

Iffy's View

Iffy's view on Meriva

Meriva is the island play I can actually put numbers against — freehold, published sizes, a dated completion and a printed plan — which already puts it ahead of most Dubai Islands launches for a serious buyer. It suits patient, payment-plan-led investors and design-led end-users buying ahead of need. It does not suit anyone needing income before 2030 or uncomfortable with a district still under construction. Before reserving I would compare entry pricing per square foot against Emaar Beachfront resale and other island launches, confirm the view line of the specific stack, and pressure-test the 2030 date against contractual remedies. Buy the plan, not the render.

FAQ

Meriva — frequently asked questions

Where is The Meriva Collection?

On the beachfront of Dubai Islands, Nakheel's five-island masterplan off the Deira coast. The brochure prints no drive times; approximately, and traffic-dependent, Gold Souq is ~10 minutes and DXB Airport ~15–20 minutes via the 2025 bridge connections.

Who is the developer?

Ellington Properties — a design-led Dubai developer describing Meriva as its most ambitious waterfront community to date.

Is Meriva freehold?

Yes — the brochure states freehold ownership, with units registered with the DLD through Ellington's booking process. Verify the specific unit's registration during diligence.

When is completion?

The developer states an anticipated completion of Q2 2030. Treat it as anticipated, not contractual, until you have the SPA — and check the contract's provisions on date movement.

What unit types and sizes are available?

One- to four-bedroom residences and penthouses in the twin towers, with published sizes from 832 sq ft (1-bed) to 4,648 sq ft (4-bed penthouse). The wider collection also markets signature 3–5 bed penthouses.

What is the payment plan?

As printed: 20% down, 5% instalments through the first year and at 20/30/40/50% construction, and 30% on completion, with an AED 40,000 booking token. Prices, availability and payment plans can change — confirm current details with Iffy before making a decision.

What is the starting price?

Unit prices are not printed in the brochure — only sizes, the booking token and the service-charge estimate. Ask Iffy for the live price list.

What are the service charges?

The developer states an anticipated AED 18 per sq ft. Build it into any yield model and verify the final figure at handover.

Is Meriva a good investment?

It is an early-cycle island entry with better-than-usual disclosure. The case depends on Dubai Islands maturing on plan, Ellington delivering to date, and your entry price — no rental history exists yet and no returns are guaranteed.

Is it suitable for families?

The amenity set is genuinely family-deep — kids' pools, play areas, a scooter track and sports courts — but the island's schools and clinics largely arrive with later phases. Families needing those today should compare established districts first.

Can overseas buyers purchase?

Yes — Dubai Islands residential projects are marketed freehold to all nationalities, with DLD registration. Verify the project's escrow and registration details during diligence.

How do I reserve a unit?

Ellington's printed process: AED 40,000 booking token with passport and contact details, then the down payment, SPA signature and DLD registration. Message Iffy first to confirm live availability and negotiate from evidence.
